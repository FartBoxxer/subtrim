# Blogger & Editor Outreach

Goal: land SubTrim on "best subscription tracker 2026" listicles and personal finance blog mentions. One batch of ~30 emails, ~5-10% reply rate, 2-5 placements.

## How to find targets (systematic)

1. Google each of these queries, take the top 10 results each:
   - `best subscription tracker apps 2026`
   - `best subscription management apps`
   - `rocket money alternatives`
   - `truebill alternatives`
   - `how to manage subscriptions`
   - `apps to save money on subscriptions`
   - `best budgeting apps 2026`

2. For each result, grab:
   - Site name
   - Author name (usually at top of post)
   - Contact email (usually in About, Contact, or footer)
   - If no email: LinkedIn DM or Twitter

3. Expected hit rate: ~60% of top sites have findable editor emails. The rest require a contact form.

## Cold email template

Subject lines (A/B two):
- `New Rocket Money alternative for your subscription tracker roundup?`
- `Free tool for your [Site Name] readers — subscription auditor`

Body:

```
Hi [First Name],

I saw your post "[Exact Post Title]" ranking on Google for "best subscription tracker apps" and wanted to share a new one that might fit.

I built SubTrim (subtrim.dev) — it's a free subscription auditor. Unlike Rocket Money or Truebill, it doesn't ask for bank credentials and doesn't charge a cut of your savings. Users answer 3 questions per subscription and get a clear "keep, cancel, or downgrade" recommendation. No signup needed for the demo.

Two things that might be useful for your readers specifically:
1. Overlap detector — flags when someone's paying for Spotify AND Apple Music (or two cloud storage plans, two VPNs, etc)
2. Shareable savings receipt — exports a PNG showing exactly what they cut and how much they saved

Happy to send screenshots, feature comparisons vs Rocket Money, or set you up with a full account for a review. No ask other than consideration.

Demo (no signup): https://subtrim.dev/demo
Direct comparison page: https://subtrim.dev/compare

Thanks,
[Your Name]
Solo dev, SubTrim
```

## What you're trading for

Most personal finance bloggers earn via affiliate links. You don't have an affiliate program yet, which hurts. Two workarounds:
1. **Manual UTM tracking** — offer custom UTM parameters so they can prove traffic to future sponsors
2. **Exclusive discount code** — when you launch paid, give them a 3-month free trial code for their readers (powerful lever once you have a paid tier)

For now, lead with "my tool is free and your readers will thank you" — the reputational upside is your pitch.

## Don't waste time on

- Mass cold outreach tools (Hunter, Apollo, Lemlist) — for 30 emails you don't need them
- Guest posting offers — editors hate "I'll write a guest post" pitches in 2026
- Contacting bloggers without a specific post reference — dead on arrival
